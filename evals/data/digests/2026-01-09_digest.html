⚡ Infrastructure & Hardware Breakthroughs

Taalas serves Llama 3.1 8B at 17,000 tokens/second with custom silicon
Canadian startup Taalas announced custom hardware that runs Llama 3.1 8B at a staggering 17,000 tok/s per user — roughly 10x faster than GPU-based inference. Their "model-on-silicon" approach merges storage and computation on a single ASIC chip, eliminating GPU dependency, liquid cooling, and complex infrastructure. Demo available at chatjimmy.ai.
ggml.ai (llama.cpp creator) joins Hugging Face to secure local AI's future
Georgi Gerganov and the ggml.ai team are joining Hugging Face, bringing llama.cpp — the backbone of tools like Ollama and LM Studio — under HF's umbrella. Since llama.cpp made it possible to run LLMs on consumer hardware in March 2023, it's become critical open-source infrastructure. The acquisition aims to ensure sustainability while keeping the projects open-source and community-driven.
